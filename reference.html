How to sort your waste in Sweden — Uppsala Vatten guide

Food Waste
Matavfall

Examples: Fruit & vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, food leftovers, eggshells, meat, fish.

What happens: Becomes biogas and biofertilizer. The biogas powers buses!

Remember: No plastic inside or around the bag. Fill only to the dotted line. Tie the bag closed.

Paper Packaging
Pappers­förpackningar

Examples: Milk cartons (Tetra Pak), juice cartons, egg cartons, cardboard packaging, paper pasta boxes.

What happens: Paper is recycled into new paper products.

Remember: Flatten cartons. Rinse liquid cartons before sorting.

Plastic Packaging
Plastförpackningar

Examples: Plastic bottles, ketchup bottles, shampoo bottles, plastic bags, chip packets, plastic film.

What happens: Plastic is recycled into new products like furniture or new plastic bags.

Remember: Empty and rinse containers. Flatten them. Put multiple small plastic bags inside one bigger bag.

Newspaper & Paper
Tidningar & papper

Examples: Daily & weekly newspapers, magazines, pocket books, envelopes, advertising leaflets, receipts.

What happens: Paper is recycled into new paper.

Remember: Never mix newspapers with plastic. Keep paper dry.

Metal Packaging
Metall­förpackningar

Examples: Aluminium cans, tin/steel soup cans, conserve cans, tubes, metal caps & lids, aluminium foil.

What happens: Metal is infinitely recyclable and melted into new products.

Remember: Empty the container before sorting. You can leave labels on.
